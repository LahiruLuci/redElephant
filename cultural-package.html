Day 2
COASTAL POINT / AREA 02

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura is one of the most significant ancient capitals of Sri Lanka, renowned for its well-preserved ruins and deep cultural heritage. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the city is sacred to Buddhists around the world and is home to ancient stupas, monasteries, and the revered Sri Maha Bodhi tree. Anuradhapura also holds importance in Hindu traditions and ancient legends, reflecting Sri Lanka’s rich and diverse historical past.

Attraction

Sri Maha Bodhi, Ruwanwelisaya, Jetavanaramaya, Ancient Monasteries

Best Buy

Cultural sightseeing, temple visits, heritage walks, ancient city exploration

Day 3
CULTURAL POINT / AREA 03

Dambulla

Dambulla is home to the largest and best-preserved cave temple complex in Sri Lanka and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This sacred site consists of over 80 documented caves, with five main caves adorned with ancient Buddhist murals and statues. The complex features a remarkable collection of 153 Buddha statues along with statues of Sri Lankan kings and deities, reflecting centuries of religious, artistic, and cultural heritage.

Attraction

Dambulla Cave Temple, Golden Temple, Ancient Buddhist Murals

Best Buy

Cave temple exploration, cultural sightseeing, heritage photography, spiritual experiences

Day 4
CULTURAL POINT / AREA 04

Polonnaruwa

Polonnaruwa served as the second ancient capital of Sri Lanka after the decline of Anuradhapura and represents the golden age of Sinhalese Buddhist civilization. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its well-preserved ruins, royal palaces, sacred temples, and impressive stone sculptures. The advanced irrigation systems and vast reservoirs built during the reign of King Parakramabahu the Great highlight the architectural brilliance and agricultural prosperity of this historic city.

Attraction

Polonnaruwa Ancient City, Gal Vihara, Royal Palace, Parakrama Samudra

Best Buy

Ancient city exploration, heritage cycling tours, temple visits, cultural photography

Day 5
CULTURAL POINT / AREA 05

Kandy

Nestled amidst the central hills, Kandy is the last royal capital of ancient Sri Lanka and remains one of the country’s most important cultural and religious cities. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Kandy is home to sacred temples, royal heritage, and traditional arts. Surrounded by misty mountains, lush greenery, and a scenic lake, the city offers a unique blend of history, spirituality, and natural beauty that continues to captivate visitors.

Attraction

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, Kandy Lake, Royal Palace, International Buddhist Museum

Best Buy

Cultural temple visits, traditional Kandyan dance performances, heritage sightseeing

Day 6
CITY POINT / AREA 06

Hatton

Hatton is located in Sri Lanka’s central highlands and is best known for its scenic tea plantations and colonial-era heritage. This region plays a vital role in Sri Lanka’s tea industry and offers visitors insight into traditional tea cultivation, estate life, and hill-country culture. Surrounded by misty mountains and lush greenery, Hatton also serves as a gateway to sacred pilgrimage routes and historic estates.

Attraction

Tea Plantations, Tea Factories, Adam’s Peak Access Route, Hill Country Estates

Best Buy

Tea estate tours, cultural village experiences, scenic walks, heritage sightseeing

Day 7
CITY POINT / AREA 07

Yala

Yala is not only renowned for its rich wildlife but also holds deep historical and cultural significance within Sri Lanka’s ancient civilization. The region is home to several ancient Buddhist temples, monastic ruins, and archaeological sites that reflect early settlement patterns and religious practices. Cultural exploration around Yala offers insight into ancient kingdoms, traditional beliefs, and the harmony between nature and heritage preserved over centuries.

Attraction

Sithulpawwa Ancient Temple, Magul Maha Viharaya, Yala Archaeological Sites

Best Buy

Ancient temple visits, cultural heritage exploration, historical site tours, nature-based heritage walks

Day 8
CITY POINT / AREA 08

Galle

Galle is a historic coastal city renowned for its rich colonial heritage and well-preserved architecture. The city is home to the iconic Galle Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site built by the Portuguese and later fortified by the Dutch. Walking through its cobblestone streets reveals ancient churches, museums, colonial-era buildings, and traditional local life, offering visitors a deep cultural and historical experience shaped by centuries of maritime trade and foreign influence.

Attraction

Galle Fort (UNESCO World Heritage Site), Dutch Reformed Church, Galle Lighthouse, Maritime Museum

Best Buy

Heritage walking tours, colonial architecture exploration, cultural sightseeing, local crafts shopping

Final Point
