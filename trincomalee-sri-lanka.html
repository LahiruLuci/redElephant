Trincomalee

Where Ancient Legends Meet Coastal Splendor

  • Home
  • Sun and Sand
  • Trincomalee

Harbor of History

Trincomalee, situated on the northeastern coast of Sri Lanka, is home to one of the world's finest natural harbors. This historic port city blends pristine beaches with a deep cultural heritage, having been a maritime center for centuries.

From the cliff-top Koneswaram Temple dedicated to Lord Shiva to the colonial-era Fort Frederick, Trincomalee offers a journey through time. Its coastline, dotted with calm bays like Uppuveli and Nilaveli, is perfect for relaxation and whale watching, making it a diverse destination for all types of travelers.

EXPLORE

Coastal Gems

01

Koneswaram Temple

Perched atop Swami Rock, this sacred Hindu temple is known as the "Temple of a Thousand Pillars" and offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Indian Ocean.

02

Pigeon Island

A short boat ride away, this national park is a marine sanctuary teeming with coral reefs, nesting rock pigeons, and diverse marine life, perfect for snorkeling.

03

Fort Frederick

Built by the Portuguese and later held by the Dutch and British, this historic fort guards the harbor entrance and houses the famous Gokana Temple.

04

Marble Beach

A stunning, secluded cove known for its marble-like smooth waters and fine white sand, offering a tranquil escape surrounded by forest.

EXPERIENCE

Things To Do

Whale Watching

Set sail into the deep blue to spot Blue Whales and Sperm Whales, which are frequently sighted off the coast of Trincomalee during the season.

Visit Hot Springs

Experience the legendary Kanniya Hot Springs, a site with seven natural wells of varying temperatures believed to have healing properties.

Lover's Leap

Visit this famous cliff near Koneswaram Temple, steeped in tragic legend and offering one of the most dramatic viewpoints in the region.

Plan Your Visit

The best time to visit Trincomalee is during the dry season from May to October. It's a perfect summer getaway.
