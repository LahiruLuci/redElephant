Hatton Sri Lanka

The Gateway to Adam's Peak

  • Home
  • Hill Country
  • Hatton

A Realm of Tea & Peaks

Hatton, nestled in the Nuwara Eliya District, is a picturesque town celebrated for its emerald tea plantations, cool climate, and colonial charm. As the primary gateway to the sacred Adam's Peak (Sri Pada), it attracts pilgrims and trekkers from around the globe seeking spiritual solace and adventure.

Beyond the peak, Hatton is home to cascading waterfalls like Devon Falls and St. Clair's Falls, often called the "Little Niagara of Sri Lanka." The town also boasts the serene Castlereagh Reservoir, offering a tranquil escape amidst misty mountains and lush greenery.

EXPLORE

Must Visit Attractions

01

Adam's Peak (Sri Pada)

A sacred mountain revered by four major religions. The summit offers the famous "sacred footprint" and stunning sunrise views that are spiritually uplifting.

02

Devon Falls

Known as the "Veil of the Valley," this magnificent waterfall plunges 97 meters and is best viewed from the dedicated observation deck along the main road.

03

Castlereagh Reservoir

A serene body of water surrounded by tea hills, perfect for boating, picnics, or staying at one of the luxury tea planter bungalows along its banks.

04

St. Clair's Falls

One of the widest waterfalls in Sri Lanka, cascading over three rock outcrops, often referred to as the "Little Niagara of Sri Lanka."

05

Tea Plantations

Explore the lush green estates and visit historic factories to witness the journey of Ceylon Tea from leaf to cup.

Things To Do

Climb Adam's Peak

Embark on a night trek with thousands of pilgrims to reach the summit by dawn and witness the spectacular shadow of the peak at sunrise.

Scenic Tea Walks

Wander through manicured tea gardens, breathe in the fresh mountain air, and watch tea pluckers at work in their vibrant attire.

Tea Factory Tours

Visit a functioning tea factory to learn about the fermentation, rolling, and drying processes, followed by a fresh cup of world-class tea.
