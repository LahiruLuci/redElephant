Yala National Park, Sri Lanka

The Kingdom of the Leopard

  • Home

Where the Wild Roams Free

Yala National Park is Sri Lanka's most visited and second-largest national park, renowned globally as one of the best places to spot the elusive Sri Lankan Leopard. Encompassing nearly 1,000 square kilometers, it features a diverse range of ecosystems, from moist monsoon forests to freshwater wetlands and untamed beaches.

Beyond the big cats, Yala is a sanctuary for magnificent elephants, sloth bears, spotted deer, and crocodiles. Its rich birdlife, with over 200 species including the colorful peacock and majestic eagles, makes it a paradise for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Must See Wildlife

01

Sri Lankan Leopard

Yala boasts one of the highest leopard densities in the world, making it the premier destination for spotting these majestic predators in their natural habitat.

02

Asian Elephants

Herds of elephants roam freely through the park's scrublands and are frequently seen bathing in the numerous waterholes and lagoons.

03

Sloth Bears

Though elusive, Yala is one of the few places where you can spot these unique bears, especially during the Palu fruit season in May and June.

04

Exotic Birdlife

From the endemic Sri Lanka Junglefowl to migratory flamingos and painted storks, Yala is a vibrant haven for ornithologists and photographers.

05

Sithulpawwa Temple

An ancient Buddhist rock temple located deep within the park, dating back over 2,000 years, offering panoramic views and a touch of spiritual history.
