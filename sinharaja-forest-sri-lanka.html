The Tropical Kingdom

Sinharaja (Lion King) Forest Reserve, the last viable primary tropical rainforest in Sri Lanka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is a treasure trove of endemic species, including trees, insects, amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals.

Unlike the dry zone parks, Sinharaja is a wet zone wonder. It's not about seeing herds of elephants, but about discovering the intricate web of life in a rainforest, from the vibrant Blue Magpie to the giant tree ferns.

Bird Waves

Experience the unique phenomenon of 'Mixed Species Feeding Flocks', where different bird species move together to forage.

02

Hidden Waterfalls

Trek to serene cascades like Kekuna Ella and Pathan Oya, tucked away deep within the verdant jungle.

03

Rare Endemics

Look out for the Sri Lanka Blue Magpie, Red-faced Malkoha, and the Green Pit Viper, exclusive to this ecosystem.

04

Giant Flora

Walk among towering Dipterocarp trees and giant ferns that create a cathedral-like canopy overhead.
