Nuwara Eliya Sri Lanka

Where Little England Meets the Tropics

  • Home
  • Hill Country
  • Nuwara Eliya

Sri Lanka's Cool Retreat

Often referred to as 'Little England', Nuwara Eliya is a picturesque hill station nestled amidst the misty peaks of Sri Lanka's central highlands. With its colonial-era bungalows, well-tended hedgerows, and pretty rose gardens, it offers a stark atmosphere contrast to the tropical warmth of the coast.

Situated at an elevation of 1,868 meters, this town is the heart of Sri Lanka's tea industry. Endless rolling hills carpeted in lush green tea bushes surround the city, interspersed with cascading waterfalls and misty forests, making it a haven for nature lovers and those seeking a cool escape.

EXPLORE

Must Visit Attractions

01

Lake Gregory

A historic man-made lake created in 1873, perfect for water sports, boat rides, and leisurely walks along its scenic shores in the cool mountain air.

02

Victoria Park

One of the country's diverse urban parks, originally the research field of Hakgala Botanical Garden, now a lovely space with exotic trees and vibrant flower beds.

03

Horton Plains

A UNESCO World Heritage site and protected area in the central highlands, famous for World's End—a sheer precipice with a 870m drop offering stunning views.

04

Hakgala Botanical Garden

Established in 1861, this garden is renowned for its collection of orchids and roses, set dramatically on the slopes of the Hakgala Rock.

05

Tea Estates

Visit iconic tea factories like Pedro or Damro to witness the tea-making process and sample some of the finest Ceylon tea right where it's grown.

EXPERIENCE

Things To Do

Hike to World's End

Embark on an early morning trek through the misty grasslands of Horton Plains to reach World's End before the clouds roll in, spotting Sambar deer along the way.

High Tea Experience

Indulge in a traditional colonial-style High Tea at the Grand Hotel or Hill Club, complete with scones, sandwiches, and premium Ceylon tea in elegant surroundings.

Golfing & Horse Riding

Play a round at the historic 18-hole Nuwara Eliya Golf Club or enjoy horse riding experiences near the racecourse, embracing the town's colonial sporting heritage.

Plan Your Visit

Nuwara Eliya is a year-round destination but is "in season" during April. Pack warm clothes as evenings can get quite chilly.
